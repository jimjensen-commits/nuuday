Overview

Nuuday A/S is Denmark's largest connectivity, communications and entertainment service provider — the customer-facing arm of TDC Group (TDC NET owns infrastructure). Operating 6 sub-brands across consumer and B2B, Nuuday serves 5.9 M customer relations and 1.8 M business customers. The company is executing one of Europe's most ambitious telco IT overhauls: replacing a 40-year-old Columbus mainframe with the cloud-native Dawn platform (Netcracker BSS/OSS + TCS hybrid cloud). As Nuuday enters the "harder next phase" — B2B/fixed-line migration, NIS2 compliance, and scaled AI — Elastic is positioned to provide critical observability, security analytics, search, and AI infrastructure.

AI & Data/Analytics

Nuuday has a formal Responsible AI Policy (human-centric, transparent, fair, secure, GDPR-compliant) and demonstrated AI maturity with Cognigy chatbots (13% call reduction), speech analytics, and digital sign-up optimization. The "new IT platform" initiative creates a window to embed Elastic as foundational AI infrastructure.

Responsible AI Policy

Five core principles govern all AI: Human-Centric Use (humans in control), Transparency (clear AI disclosure), Fairness (bias auditing), Security (vulnerability management), and Data Privacy (GDPR + purpose limitation). This constrains black-box models and autonomous decisions but enables employee GenAI, chatbots, predictive analytics, and process automation.

Data Platform Maturity

Central data platform: developing. Customer Data Platform: partial. Analytics tooling: operational (Cognigy, speech analytics). AI/ML infrastructure: growing (Netcracker AI module, CloudKey). Data governance: established (Responsible AI Policy, GDPR via CloudKey).

Current AI Use Cases

In production: AI chatbots (Cognigy) — 13% call reduction; Speech analytics — call quality & sentiment; Digital sign-up optimization; Easy brand full digital transformation (call center shutdown). Emerging/B2B: CloudKey — encrypted GDPR-compliant AI for B2B; Security Suite — AI-powered threat detection (TDC Erhverv).

Security & Regulatory

Nuuday is a critical infrastructure provider automatically classified under NIS2. With NIS2 taking effect July 2025, EU AI Act requirements for AI governance, and strict GDPR/ePrivacy telecom obligations, there is board-level urgency for enhanced security monitoring and compliance tooling.

Nuuday Security Posture

Responsible AI Policy mandates cybersecurity standards, regular assessments, continuous monitoring, and rigorous testing for all AI systems. Security products: TDC Erhverv Security Suite (B2B cyber protection), CloudKey (encrypted, GDPR-compliant cloud/AI), and critical infrastructure classification under NIS2.

Talk Tracks for the Meeting

Talk Track 1 — Unified Observability for the "Harder Next Phase"

CTIO / IT Ops Observability

Context

B2C mobile migration delivered great results. Now the harder B2B/fixed-line phase begins — more complex systems, higher customer-impact risk, continued hybrid operations with both Columbus and Dawn running in parallel.

Hypothesis of Pain

Maintaining visibility across legacy and new cloud-native stacks becomes exponentially harder. Siloed monitoring creates blind spots and slow incident response during critical migration windows.

How Elastic Maps

Elastic Observability provides unified visibility — logs, metrics, traces, APM — in a single searchable platform. Correlates data across legacy and cloud-native systems. APM auto-discovers service dependencies in microservices.

Suggested Questions

  1. "When you have an incident spanning TCS cloud, Netcracker apps, and legacy systems, how quickly can your teams correlate events across these boundaries today?"
  2. "With NIS2 approaching in July, how confident are you in detecting and investigating security events across this hybrid environment?"

Talk Track 2 — AI Infrastructure for the Responsible AI Future

Data & AI / CTIO Search + AI

Context

Demonstrated AI maturity: Cognigy chatbots, responsible AI policy, 13% call reduction. Exploring GenAI with EU AI Act compliance positioning.

Hypothesis of Pain

Scaling AI requires infrastructure for semantic search, RAG, and AI assistants — while maintaining GDPR compliance and EU AI Act alignment. Separate vector databases create data movement risks.

How Elastic Maps

Native vector database + semantic search in the same observability platform. RAG enables AI assistants grounded in real data — customer records, network events, tickets — within governance framework. No separate vector DB needed.

Suggested Questions

  1. "For your Cognigy chatbots, how do you ensure they have access to the most current, relevant context like real-time customer data?"
  2. "With your responsible AI policy, how are you approaching making AI decisions explainable and auditable?"

Talk Track 3 — Security Analytics for NIS2 Compliance

CISO / CTIO Security SIEM

Context

Critical infrastructure provider serving 4.1M households + 1.8M businesses. NIS2 effective July 2025. Dawn migration = moving from controlled mainframe to distributed cloud-native with thousands of APIs.

Hypothesis of Pain

NIS2 requires enhanced threat detection, incident response, and monitoring. Transition period creates expanded attack surface across legacy + cloud + hybrid integrations + third-party connections. Traditional SIEM may struggle with volume.

How Elastic Maps

Modern, open SIEM scaling to massive cloud-native data volumes while supporting legacy logs. Pre-built detection rules, AI-powered alert prioritization. Unified platform with Observability for security + performance visibility together.

Suggested Questions

  1. "How confident are you in detecting threats that move laterally between legacy systems and the new cloud environment?"
  2. "For NIS2, how are you planning to correlate security events across TCS, Netcracker, and your own operations?"
